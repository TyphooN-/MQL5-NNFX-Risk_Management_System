**Target:** $53.00 (4.236 fib extension)
**Thesis:** AI + crypto energy demand is growing faster than supply on a grid that can't keep up. Natural gas is the only bridge fuel that works NOW. Trump's energy dominance agenda accelerates both supply AND demand — but demand wins.

## The Demand Side: Structural, Permanent, Accelerating

### AI Data Centers

The single largest new source of energy demand in US history is being built right now.

- **$500B+ in data center investment in 2026** — up from $325B forecast just a year earlier
- Data center NG demand: **0.8 Bcf/d → 2.5 Bcf/d by end of 2026** — 3x growth in one year
- Projected **12 Bcf/d of additional gas demand by 2030** — a 33% increase on current ~37 Bcf/d baseline
- Every major AI lab (OpenAI, Google, Meta, xAI, Anthropic) is building massive compute clusters simultaneously
- This is not cyclical — every model generation requires more compute, and inference demand only grows

**The grid can't deliver.** Data centers face up to a **5-year wait** to get energy from local utilities. So they're going "behind the meter" — building their own natural gas power plants on-site. New gas plant proposals in the US **tripled in 2025**. This is permanent baseload demand that bypasses the grid entirely.

### Crypto Mining

Bitcoin mining operations increasingly use natural gas turbines for power:

- **Stranded/flared gas** at wellheads now has a buyer — mobile mining containers convert waste gas to Bitcoin
- **Behind-the-meter** operations bypass grid constraints entirely
- Post-China ban, US dominates Bitcoin hashrate — Texas, North Dakota, Wyoming, Pennsylvania
- **Self-correcting demand floor**: when natgas is cheap, mining becomes MORE profitable → more miners deploy → more gas demand → price support

This creates a structural price floor at ~$2.00-2.50 that didn't exist before ~2020. As US crypto mining continues to grow, this floor strengthens over time.

### The Grid Problem

The US power grid is decades behind and cannot support the simultaneous demands of:
1. AI data center buildout (unprecedented baseload growth)
2. Crypto mining (continuous, location-flexible demand)
3. Electrification of transport (EVs adding load)
4. Normal population/economic growth

Renewables cannot deliver baseload at scale. Solar and wind are intermittent — data centers need 24/7/365 power. Nuclear takes 10+ years to permit and build. **Natural gas is the only fuel that can be deployed at scale, on schedule, to meet demand that is growing NOW.**

- **LNG export terms extended to 2050** (was 20-year terms) — long-term certainty for exporters

### Production vs Export Growth

This is where the thesis gets interesting:

| Metric | 2025 | 2026 | 2027 | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| US NG production | ~118 Bcf/d | 120.8 Bcf/d | 122.3 Bcf/d | **+2% / year** |
| LNG exports | ~15.4 Bcf/d | 16.4 Bcf/d | 18.1 Bcf/d | **+6% / year** |
| Data center demand | 0.8 Bcf/d | 2.5 Bcf/d | ~5 Bcf/d (est.) | **+200% / year** |

**Production grows 2%. Exports grow 6%. Data center demand grows 200%.** The math doesn't work. Something has to give — and the thing that gives is price.

The US now produces as much natural gas as **Russia, Iran, and China combined** (108 Bcf/d). And it's still not enough.

### The Trump Paradox

Trump's energy dominance agenda simultaneously:
1. **Increases supply** — more drilling, more permits, more production
2. **Increases demand even faster** — more LNG exports, more data centers (which he champions), more crypto mining (which he supports)
3. **Raises domestic prices** — exporting 4 Bcf/d more than Biden means 4 Bcf/d less for domestic consumption
4. **Creates political cover** — he can blame the grid, blame Biden's legacy infrastructure, while championing the industries (AI, crypto) that are driving demand

**He is simultaneously the cause AND the political beneficiary of rising gas prices.** He exports more, AI eats the rest, the grid can't keep up, prices rise, and he gets to position himself as the savior who will fix the grid — by building more gas infrastructure.

## Seasonal Amplifier

Natural gas follows a reliable seasonal cycle that amplifies the structural thesis:

```
Spring (Mar-Apr): Seasonal low — heating demand drops, production steady → ENTRY ZONE
Summer (Jun-Aug): Moderate demand — cooling/power generation + data center buildout
Fall (Sep-Nov):   Building season — storage injections, LNG export ramp
Winter (Dec-Feb): Seasonal high — heating demand peaks, cold snaps spike price → EXIT ZONE
```

## Geopolitical Catalysts

### Strait of Hormuz Crisis (Active — 2026)

The 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis has disrupted Qatar LNG exports — **20% of global LNG supply**. While US domestic gas is relatively insulated (export terminals at capacity), prolonged disruption:
- Diverts global LNG demand toward US exports
- Tightens global gas markets
- Creates urgency for more US LNG export capacity (which Trump is fast-tracking)

### Europe's Energy Dependence

Post-Ukraine, Europe has permanently shifted from Russian pipeline gas to LNG. US LNG exports to Europe are a strategic lever — and Trump is using it. Every cubic foot exported to Europe is one less available domestically.
